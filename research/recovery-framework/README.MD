# Recovery Framework

## Purpose

The Recovery Framework provides a conceptual foundation for understanding how people preserve or regain agency while living with chronic illness, repeated trauma, and other high-stress environments.

This framework investigates recovery as an adaptive process occurring across individuals, relationships, institutions, and technology. It asks how people interpret signals, coordinate support, navigate complex systems, and make meaningful decisions under persistent uncertainty.

The framework integrates established research across neuroscience, psychology, behavioral medicine, health services research, and systems science while proposing new concepts intended for empirical investigation through participatory research.

## Initial Research Questions

- The framework currently investigates questions including:
- Under what conditions does distress originating in one domain become attributed to another?
-What information can be made directly interpretable to patients and communities without replacing clinical expertise?
- What assumptions do existing digital therapeutics make about recovery?
- Does improving symptom management without improving patient agency produce durable recovery?
- How can co-regulation networks be measured?
- How can institutional friction be quantified?
- Can recovery be understood as a property of a person and their surrounding network rather than the individual alone?
